Nutrition for Fat Loss & Muscle Gain

🥗

Protein is Key

Aim for 0.7-1g of protein per pound of body weight daily (about 85-125g). This supports muscle building while losing fat. Good sources: chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, beans, and tofu.

🍎

Calorie Deficit (Small & Sustainable)

To lose 5 lbs safely, aim for a modest 250-300 calorie deficit per day. This equals about 1 lb lost every 2 weeks. Don't cut too drastically — your body needs fuel for workouts!

🥬

Eat More Vegetables

Fill half your plate with vegetables. They're low in calories, high in fiber, and packed with nutrients that support recovery and overall health.

💧

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 8 glasses (64 oz) of water daily. Dehydration can be mistaken for hunger and negatively affects workout performance.

⏰

Meal Timing

Eat a small meal with protein and carbs 1-2 hours before working out, and have protein within 30-60 minutes after. This maximizes muscle recovery.

Recovery & Injury Prevention

🛏

Prioritize Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep. This is when your muscles repair and grow. Poor sleep increases cortisol, which promotes fat storage.

🧘

Always Warm Up & Cool Down

Never skip warming up! At 50, your muscles and joints need extra preparation. 5 minutes of light movement before and stretching after each workout prevents injury.

💪

Listen to Your Body

Muscle soreness is normal, but sharp pain is not. If something hurts (beyond normal soreness), rest that area and modify exercises. It's better to take a day off than be out for weeks.

🔄

Rest Days Matter

Your muscles grow during rest, not during exercise. The cardio days between strength training give your muscles time to recover while still keeping you active.

🦴

Bone Health

Weight-bearing exercises (like the ones in your program) help maintain bone density, which is especially important after 50. Consider a calcium and vitamin D supplement — talk to your doctor.

General Form Tips

🎯

Start Light

Begin with lighter dumbbells (3-8 lbs) and focus on perfect form. You can always increase weight once the movement feels comfortable. Proper form prevents injury.

🕒

Control the Movement

Move slowly and deliberately. Count 2-3 seconds on the way up and 2-3 seconds on the way down. This keeps tension on the muscle and reduces joint stress.

💨

Breathe Properly

Exhale during the hard part (lifting) and inhale during the easy part (lowering). Never hold your breath — this can spike blood pressure.

📈

Progressive Overload

Gradually increase difficulty every 2-3 weeks. Add 1-2 more reps, use slightly heavier dumbbells, or take shorter rest periods. Small progress adds up!
